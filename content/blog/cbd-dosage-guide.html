CBD Dosage Guide: How Much CBD Should I Take?

April 4, 2026 11 min read

The single most common question we hear — at the farmers market, in emails, even from friends at dinner — is some version of "how much of this stuff do I actually take?" It is a fair question. And honestly, the answer is less straightforward than we wish it were.

CBD is not like ibuprofen where there is a standard two-pill dose printed on the bottle. Bodies are different. Goals are different. The product you are using matters. Your lunch matters (seriously — more on that later). What we can do is walk you through what the research says, what we have seen with our own customers over the years, and how to think about finding your own starting point without overthinking it.

We are not doctors. Brynn has her nutritional therapy certificate, and Ben grows the hemp, but neither of us is writing prescriptions. What follows is education based on published research — not medical advice. If you are managing a health condition or taking medication, talk to your doctor first. We mean that.

Why There Is No Universal CBD Dose

If you have searched "CBD dosage" before landing here, you have probably noticed that every website gives you a different number. That is not because everyone is making it up. It is because CBD genuinely affects people differently based on several real factors.

Body weight is the most obvious one. A 120-pound person and a 220-pound person are not going to respond to the same amount of CBD identically. Research generally suggests somewhere around 0.1 to 0.3mg of CBD per pound of body weight as a ballpark starting range.

What you are using it for matters quite a bit. Someone adding CBD to their morning routine for general wellness is working with a different dose range than someone dealing with significant physical discomfort.

Your individual biology plays a role too. Metabolism, body composition, your endocannabinoid system's baseline tone — these are not things you can measure at home, but they explain why your neighbor swears by 10mg and your coworker needs 40mg for similar results.

Tolerance is real. If you have been using CBD consistently for months, you may find your effective dose shifts over time. Some people actually find they need less over time, not more.

Starting Doses: The Low-and-Slow Approach

We tell every new customer the same thing: start low, go slow.

Most research points to 10 to 20mg per day as a sensible starting dose for adults. Some people begin even lower — as little as 5mg.

Here is the general framework:

  1. Pick your starting dose — 10mg is a solid middle ground for most adults
  2. Stay there for 3 to 5 days — give your body time to respond
  3. Adjust in small increments — go up by 5 to 10mg if you are not noticing the effects you want
  4. Keep notes — even a quick note on your phone about how you felt that day helps you track what is actually working

With our 600mg full spectrum oil, one full dropper gives you about 20mg — so half a dropper is roughly 10mg. That is a clean starting point.

The goal is not to take the most CBD possible. The goal is to find the lowest effective dose — the smallest amount that gives you the results you are looking for.

Dose Ranges by Purpose: What Research Suggests

We need to be clear here: we are not prescribing doses for conditions. What we can do is share what published studies and clinical research have found.

General wellness and everyday stress: Studies suggest 10 to 20mg daily as a low-dose starting range. This is where most of our customers land for daily use.

Sleep support: Research suggests starting at 10 to 20mg taken in the evening. One clinical study found 160mg was associated with improved sleep duration — a significantly higher dose than most people start with.

Anxious feelings: Everyday users commonly report 10 to 20mg as helpful. However, clinical evidence looking at more pronounced effects has used doses in the 300 to 400mg range. That is a big gap, which tells you something about how individual this really is.

Physical discomfort: Research suggests 30 to 60mg daily as a starting range. Our salves can complement an oral dose for targeted areas.

How Your Product Type Affects Dosing

Sublingual oils (held under the tongue for 60 to 90 seconds) absorb through the mucous membranes and typically take effect within 15 to 30 minutes. This is our most popular format because it is easy to control your dose drop by drop.

Edibles and gummies pass through your digestive system first, which means they take longer to kick in — usually 45 to 90 minutes. The trade-off is that effects tend to last longer. Just keep in mind that "I do not feel anything yet" at the 30-minute mark does not mean you should take another one.

Topical salves do not enter your bloodstream in meaningful amounts — they work locally where you apply them. Dosing is less about milligrams and more about application.

One factor people overlook: food. Research has shown that taking CBD with a meal that includes dietary fat significantly increases absorption. A handful of almonds, some avocado toast, eggs — anything with fat content helps.

Understanding Milligrams: Per Serving vs Per Bottle

The big number on the bottle — say 600mg or 1200mg — is the total CBD in the entire container. It is not what you get per dose.

To figure out your per-serving dose, you divide:

  • Our 600mg oil (30mL bottle): 600 / 30 = 20mg per full dropper (1mL)
  • Our 1200mg oil (30mL bottle): 1200 / 30 = 40mg per full dropper

When comparing products — ours or anyone else's — always look at the per-serving milligrams, not the total bottle number.

Safety and Side Effects

CBD has a solid safety profile based on the available research. Studies suggest it is generally well tolerated at doses up to 1500mg per day — far above what most people use.

Common side effects include dry mouth, drowsiness, changes in appetite, and occasional digestive changes. At doses above 300mg daily, some studies have observed elevated liver enzymes.

Drug interactions are real. CBD is processed by the same liver enzymes as many common medications. If you take prescription drugs — especially anticonvulsants or blood thinners — please have a conversation with your provider before adding CBD.

We would rather you feel confident and safe than sell you an extra bottle.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many mg of CBD should a beginner take?
Most research suggests starting with 10 to 20mg per day. We recommend beginning at the lower end and increasing gradually over a week or two.
Can you take too much CBD?
While CBD is generally considered safe at doses up to 1500mg per day in research settings, higher doses have been associated with side effects like drowsiness and elevated liver enzymes. Finding the lowest effective dose is a smarter approach.
How long does it take for CBD oil to work?
Sublingual CBD oil typically takes 15 to 30 minutes. Edibles and gummies take 45 to 90 minutes because they pass through your digestive system first.
Does body weight affect CBD dosage?
Yes. Research suggests using 0.1 to 0.3mg of CBD per pound of body weight as a general starting framework.
Should I take CBD with food?
Research shows that taking CBD with a meal containing dietary fat significantly increases absorption.
