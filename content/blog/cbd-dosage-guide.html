CBD Dosage Guide: How Much CBD Should I Take?

CBD Education April 4, 2026 11 min read

The single most common question we hear — at the farmers market, in emails, even from friends at dinner — is some version of "how much of this stuff do I actually take?" It is a fair question. And honestly, the answer is less straightforward than we wish it were.

CBD is not like ibuprofen where there is a standard two-pill dose printed on the bottle. Bodies are different. Goals are different. The product you are using matters. Your lunch matters (seriously — more on that later).

We are not doctors. Brynn has her nutritional therapy certificate, and Ben grows the hemp, but neither of us is writing prescriptions. What follows is education based on published research — not medical advice. If you are managing a health condition or taking medication, talk to your doctor first. We mean that.

Why There Is No Universal CBD Dose

Several factors determine what dose works for any individual:

  • Body weight is the most obvious factor. Research suggests 0.1 to 0.3mg of CBD per pound of body weight as a starting framework.
  • What you're using it for matters. General wellness doses look different from doses studied for sleep or discomfort.
  • Your individual biology plays a role. Metabolism, endocannabinoid system tone, and even genetics affect how you process CBD.
  • Tolerance is real. Some people find their ideal dose shifts over time, though CBD tolerance tends to be slower-building than many other compounds.

Starting Doses: The Low-and-Slow Approach

Most research and clinical guidance points to 10 to 20mg per day as a reasonable starting point for most adults. The framework is simple:

  1. Pick a starting dose — 10mg is a good baseline for most people.
  2. Stay there for 3-5 days. CBD can take time to build up in your system.
  3. Adjust in 5-10mg increments if you're not noticing what you hoped for.
  4. Keep notes. Even brief ones. "10mg, morning, felt calmer by afternoon" is more useful than you'd think.

With our 600mg oil, one full dropper equals approximately 20mg of CBD. A half dropper gives you roughly 10mg — a perfect starting point.

Dose Ranges by Purpose: What Research Suggests

  • General wellness: 10-20mg daily. This is where most people start and many stay.
  • Sleep: 10-20mg in the evening. Clinical studies have used higher doses (160mg) for sleep duration, but most people find lower doses sufficient for relaxation before bed.
  • Anxious feelings: 10-20mg daily for everyday use. Clinical research on acute situational anxiety has used 300-400mg, but these are supervised study doses — not a starting point.
  • Physical discomfort: 30-60mg, often split between morning and evening. Topical application (like our salve) can complement oral use for localized issues.

How Your Product Type Affects Dosing

  • Sublingual oils (held under the tongue): 15-30 minute onset. This is the most common method and what most dosage research is based on. Hold under your tongue for 60-90 seconds before swallowing for best absorption.
  • Edibles and gummies: 45-90 minute onset. They pass through your digestive system first, so onset is slower and bioavailability is lower. You may need a slightly higher dose to achieve the same effect.
  • Topical salves: Local effect only. CBD applied to the skin doesn't enter your bloodstream in meaningful amounts — it works on cannabinoid receptors in the skin and underlying tissue. Great for targeted areas, not a replacement for oral dosing.

One thing most people miss: taking CBD with food that contains fat significantly increases absorption. A 2019 University of Minnesota study found that taking CBD with a high-fat meal increased bioavailability by up to 4-5 times compared to taking it on an empty stomach. Your morning eggs or avocado toast are doing more work than you think.

Understanding Milligrams: Per Serving vs Per Bottle

This is where the CBD industry gets confusing on purpose. When a bottle says "600mg," that's the total CBD in the entire bottle — not per serving.

  • 600mg bottle / 30mL = 20mg per full dropper
  • 1200mg bottle / 30mL = 40mg per full dropper

Always compare products by their per-serving milligrams, not the total bottle amount. A 600mg bottle at $60 (20mg per serving, 30 servings) is actually a better deal than a 1000mg bottle at $120 if the 1000mg bottle only gives you 15 servings.

Safety and Side Effects

CBD is generally considered safe. Research has used doses up to 1,500mg per day without serious adverse effects. But that doesn't mean more is always better.

Common side effects at higher doses include:

  • Dry mouth
  • Drowsiness (which can be a feature, not a bug, if you're taking it for sleep)
  • Minor appetite changes

At doses above 300mg daily, some studies have noted elevated liver enzymes. This is worth monitoring with your doctor if you're taking high doses long-term.

Drug interactions are real. CBD is processed by the same liver enzymes (CYP3A4 and CYP2C19) as many common medications. If you take anticonvulsants, blood thinners, or other medications metabolized by these pathways, talk to your doctor before starting CBD. This isn't a disclaimer — it's genuinely important.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many mg of CBD should a beginner take?

Start with 10-20mg per day. Begin at the lower end, stay there for 3-5 days, and adjust gradually in 5-10mg increments based on how you feel.

Can you take too much CBD?

Research has used doses up to 1,500mg per day without serious adverse effects. However, more isn't necessarily better — most people find their sweet spot well below 100mg daily. Side effects like drowsiness and dry mouth are more common at higher doses.

How long does CBD oil take to work?

Sublingual oils (under the tongue) typically take 15-30 minutes. Edibles and gummies take 45-90 minutes because they pass through your digestive system first.

Does body weight affect CBD dosage?

Yes. A common starting framework is 0.1 to 0.3mg of CBD per pound of body weight. A 150-pound person might start at 15mg (low) to 45mg (strong).

Should I take CBD with food?

Yes. A 2019 study found that taking CBD with dietary fat increased absorption by up to 4-5 times. Taking it with a meal or snack that contains some fat makes a meaningful difference.
