Post 4 — USDA Organic Carousel
FG
fiddlersgreenfarmlife
"Organic" gets thrown around a lot.
Here's what it actually requires.
SWIPE →
Three Years. Minimum.
Before you can even apply for certification, your land must be free of prohibited substances for 36 months. Three full growing seasons of doing everything right with nothing to show for it on a label.
Every Input Is Documented
Every seed. Every amendment. Every spray — even the approved ones. An organic farm keeps more paperwork than most offices. Inspectors review it all. Annually.
No Synthetic Shortcuts
No synthetic fertilizers. No conventional pesticides. No GMO seed. When a pest problem hits, you solve it with biology, not chemistry. It's slower. It's harder. It works.
The Inspector Shows Up
Once a year, a third-party inspector walks the farm. Checks the fields. Checks the records. Checks the buffer zones between us and conventional neighbors. Nothing is taken on faith.
So When You See Our Label —
Know that it's not marketing. It's a paper trail three years deep, audited every year, backed by work you can see in the soil. When you choose certified organic, you're choosing accountability. You're part of making this standard worth something.
