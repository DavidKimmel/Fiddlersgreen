Key Insights

Critically Low Posting Frequency

Only 3 posts in the last 90 days, with an average gap of ~5 weeks between posts. This severely limits algorithmic reach and follower retention.

Community Content Dominates

The Thanksgiving gratitude post pulled 92 likes — the highest organic engagement. Farm life content consistently outperforms product and promo posts.

Sale Graphics Underperform

Both promotional posts averaged just 6 likes. Generic sale graphics are the lowest-performing content type and waste posting slots.

Local Collabs Drive Results

Brewery, soap maker, and co-op partnerships consistently hit 29-47 total engagement. Cross-audience exposure works well for this account.

Giveaway Engagement Is Inflated

The honey giveaway hit 117 total engagement, but 76 were contest-entry comments. Useful for reach spikes, but shouldn't be the primary strategy.

Recommendations

  • 1
    Increase posting frequency to 3x/week minimum. The single biggest issue. Consistency signals relevance to the algorithm and keeps the audience engaged.
  • 2
    Lead with farm life and community content. Behind-the-scenes moments, animal photos, harvest updates, and gratitude posts generate the highest organic engagement. Make this 60% of content.
  • 3
    Double down on local collabs. Brewery, soap maker, co-op, and market partnerships consistently outperform. Aim for 1-2 collab posts per month.
  • 4
    Stop posting generic sale graphics. They average 6 likes. Instead, weave promotions into storytelling posts with the product as a secondary element.
  • 5
    Use product restocks as secondary content. Restocks get moderate engagement (9-20 likes). Pair them with farm stories or customer testimonials.
  • 6
    Launch a recurring content series. A weekly "What's Growing" or "Farm Friday" gives the audience a reason to check back and builds posting discipline.
