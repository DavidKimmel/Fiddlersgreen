Key Insights

Critically Low Posting Frequency

Only 3 posts in the last 90 days, with an average gap of ~5.3 weeks between posts. This kills algorithmic reach and follower retention. The account is effectively dormant in Instagram's eyes.

Community Content Dominates

The Thanksgiving gratitude post pulled 92 likes — the highest organic engagement by far. Farm life content (bluebirds post: 27 likes) also outperforms product and promo posts. Authenticity wins.

Sale Graphics Flatline

Both Black Friday / promo posts averaged just 6 likes with near-zero comments. Generic sale graphics are the lowest-performing content type and actively waste posting slots.

Local Collabs Are Proven Winners

The brewery collab (29 likes), soap collab (47 total engagement), and co-op feature (30 total) consistently outperform product-only posts. Cross-audience exposure drives real engagement.

Giveaway Engagement Is Artificial

The honey giveaway hit 117 total engagement, but 76 of that was comments (contest entries). While useful for reach spikes, this doesn't reflect sustained organic interest and shouldn't be the primary strategy.

Recommendations

  • 01
    Increase posting frequency to 3x/week minimum. The single biggest issue. Even 2x/week would be a massive improvement. Consistency signals relevance to the algorithm and keeps the audience engaged.
  • 02
    Lead with farm life and community content. Behind-the-scenes farm moments, animal photos, harvest updates, and gratitude posts generate the highest organic engagement. Make this 60% of content.
  • 03
    Double down on local collabs. Brewery, soap maker, co-op, and market partnerships consistently outperform. Aim for 1-2 collab posts per month — they tap into partner audiences.
  • 04
    Stop posting generic sale graphics. They average 6 likes and hurt overall engagement rate. Instead, weave promotions into storytelling posts (e.g., "why we grow Turkey Red wheat" + mention the restock).
  • 05
    Use product restocks as secondary content, not primary. Restocks get moderate engagement (9-20 likes). Pair them with farm stories or customer testimonials rather than standalone announcements.
  • 06
    Launch a recurring content series. A weekly "What's Growing Wednesday" or "Farm Friday" gives the audience a reason to check back and builds posting discipline. Series content is easier to plan and batch.
